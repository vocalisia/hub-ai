# Google E-E-A-T 2026: How AI Strengthens Your Authority and Ranking

When Google evaluates the quality of a web page to determine its positioning, it uses thousands of signals. But among all these signals, one framework has dominated since 2014 and has taken on even greater importance with the AI era: E-E-A-T. Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness — these four dimensions determine whether Google perceives you as a reliable source worthy of promotion.

In 2026, E-E-A-T is no longer just a quality criterion for Google's human evaluators who manually assess sites — it is a set of signals that Google's AI algorithms measure and weight automatically. And paradoxically, artificial intelligence can become your best ally for reinforcing each of these dimensions.

## Understanding E-E-A-T: Much More Than an Acronym

### The History of E-E-A-T: From E-A-T to E-E-A-T

Google introduced E-A-T (Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) in its Quality Rater Guidelines in 2014. This framework was initially intended for human evaluators (Quality Raters) who rate the quality of web pages to train algorithms.

In December 2022, Google added a second "E" for "Experience" — a strong signal that first-hand experience is now valued as much as academic expertise. This evolution responds to a reality: in a world of information overabundance, content created by someone who has **lived** the experience they describe has particular value that algorithms must learn to detect.

### YMYL Sectors: When E-E-A-T Is Critical

E-E-A-T applies to all sites, but its importance is maximum for YMYL — "Your Money or Your Life" topics. These domains, where incorrect information can have serious consequences on an individual's health, finances, or safety, are subject to the strictest E-E-A-T standards:

- Health and medicine
- Personal finance and investment
- Law and legal
- Safety and emergencies
- News and current events

For these sectors, Google applies particularly demanding evaluation criteria. But even outside YMYL, E-E-A-T is a significant differentiator.

## The 4 Dimensions of E-E-A-T Decoded

### E as Experience: First-Hand Experience

Experience is the most recent and subtlest dimension of E-E-A-T. It answers a simple question: has the content author personally lived, used, or experienced what they are talking about?

**Positive experience signals**:
- Specific and verifiable personal anecdotes
- Original photos or videos of the lived experience
- Concrete details that only someone who lived the situation could know
- Content updates reflecting ongoing experience over time

**How AI can strengthen this signal**:
- Analyze your existing content to identify experiential gaps
- Structure your experience narratives more credibly and in more detail
- Suggest the types of evidence to integrate (photos, data, testimonials)
- Automate the collection and integration of authentic customer reviews

AI cannot invent experience — but it can help you better showcase it in your content.

### E as Expertise: Subject Mastery

Expertise concerns the depth of knowledge demonstrated in the content. It can be formal (degrees, certifications) or informal (recognized self-taught expert, practitioner with years of experience).

**Positive expertise signals**:
- Complete author biographies with verifiable credentials
- Author LinkedIn profiles linked from articles
- Certifications and degrees mentioned
- References to original work (studies conducted, training created)
- Content that goes beyond the "Wikipedia-level" of a topic

**How AI strengthens expertise**:
- Identify angles not addressed by competitors to differentiate yourself
- Enrich content with automatically sourced recent data
- Generate comparative analyses that only an expert can validate
- Detect claims that lack sources or evidence

AI expertise does not replace human expertise — it amplifies and makes it more visible.

### A as Authoritativeness: Recognized Authority

Authority is the most external dimension — it is built in the eyes of others. A site is authoritative when other recognized sources cite it, recommend it, and refer to it.

**Positive authority signals**:
- Backlinks from recognized sites in your sector
- Mentions in specialized publications (press, studies, reports)
- Citations in official sources (institutions, associations)
- Conference invitations, media interviews
- Number and quality of shares on professional social networks

**How AI strengthens authority**:
- Identify the most strategic backlink opportunities
- Create "linkable" content (studies, tools, infographics) that naturally attracts links
- Automatically monitor your new web mentions
- Analyze your competitors' backlink profile to identify targets

The experts at [trustly-ai.com](https://trustly-ai.com) have developed a specific methodology for building online authority in the AI era — integrating both traditional link building signals and the new citation signals in generative engines. Their approach shows that E-E-A-T and presence in generative AIs are complementary, not separate objectives.

### T as Trustworthiness: Reliability and Trust

Trustworthiness is the fundamental dimension — Google states it is the most important of the four. It concerns the security, transparency, and perceived honesty of your site.

**Positive trustworthiness signals**:
- Active HTTPS with up-to-date valid certificate
- Complete and accessible privacy policy
- Legal notices (company, registration number, director)
- Clear terms and conditions
- Sources cited for each factual claim
- Authentic and unfiltered customer reviews
- Absence of proven factual errors in content

**How AI strengthens trustworthiness**:
- Automatically audit pages to detect unsourced information
- Verify the consistency of legal information across the site
- Monitor customer reviews and alert on responses needed
- Detect biases or potentially controversial claims before publication

## E-E-A-T and Generative AIs: An Underestimated Synergy

### Why E-E-A-T Influences Citation in AIs

The LLMs (Large Language Models) that power ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini were trained on text corpora in which high-quality sources (sites with strong E-E-A-T) are overrepresented. Consequently, sites with solid E-E-A-T have a higher probability of being cited in AI-generated responses.

This correlation is not accidental — it is an emergent property of the training process. LLMs "prefer" sources that are themselves cited by other reliable sources, that present identified and credible authors, and that produce factual and well-structured content. Exactly the signals of E-E-A-T.

### The New Signal: Citability in Generative AIs

In 2026, a new dimension is implicitly added to E-E-A-T: **citability in generative AIs**. Content "citable" by AIs is content that:

- Contains precise and verifiable factual claims
- Is structured to facilitate information extraction (lists, tables, FAQ)
- Presents an identifiable author with recognized expertise
- Is accessible to all (no paywall, no heavy JavaScript)
- Is regularly updated

Analyses from [trustly-ai.com](https://trustly-ai.com) show that sites appearing in Perplexity responses have on average a Moz Domain Authority (DA) 15 points higher than those that don't appear — a strong correlation with traditional authority signals.

## Actionable E-E-A-T Checklist for 2026

### Experience Checklist

- [ ] Each article involving a recommendation is signed by someone who has experienced the recommended thing
- [ ] Anecdotes or concrete examples from real experiences are integrated into the content
- [ ] Original photos, videos, or data illustrate the lived experience
- [ ] Both creation AND update dates are visible on each article
- [ ] Authentic customer reviews are highlighted on product/service pages

### Expertise Checklist

- [ ] Each author has a complete biography with verifiable credentials
- [ ] Author LinkedIn profiles are linked from author pages
- [ ] Relevant certifications and degrees are mentioned
- [ ] Articles contain information beyond the "Wikipedia level" of the topic
- [ ] Factual claims are sourced with links to primary sources

### Authoritativeness Checklist

- [ ] A backlink audit has been conducted to identify existing quality links
- [ ] An active link building strategy is in place (guest posting, partnerships, digital PR)
- [ ] Unlinked brand mentions are monitored and link requests are made
- [ ] At least 2 recognized external publications cite your site or author
- [ ] Your Wikipedia (or Wikidata) entry exists if your brand warrants it

### Trustworthiness Checklist

- [ ] Active HTTPS with valid SSL certificate
- [ ] GDPR-compliant privacy policy accessible from the footer
- [ ] Complete legal notices with company identity
- [ ] Clear terms and conditions for e-commerce or SaaS sites
- [ ] Functional contact page with multiple contact methods
- [ ] Negative customer reviews are visible and receive professional responses
- [ ] No misleading or deceptive claims in content

**Ahrefs or Semrush**: Monitor your Domain Rating (DR) / Domain Authority (DA) and the evolution of your backlink profile. These metrics are proxies of your authority.

**Google Alerts**: Create alerts on your brand, your author name, and your core topics to capture all web mentions.

**Generate expert questions**: Submit your draft to an AI asking "What questions would a skeptical expert ask about this content?" The responses reveal the weak angles of your content.

## E-E-A-T Mistakes to Absolutely Avoid

**Publishing under pseudonyms or without identified author**: Google cannot evaluate the expertise of an anonymous author. Even for content generated with AI assistance, an identified human author must supervise and validate.

**Copy-pasting statistical data without sourcing it**: Unsourced figures are a signal of low trustworthiness. Always cite the primary source (study, official body, company report).

**Neglecting "institutional" pages**: About, Team, Privacy Policy, and Legal Notice pages are directly evaluated by Google's Quality Raters. They must be complete, accurate, and regularly updated.

**Over-optimizing at the expense of quality**: Content stuffed with keywords, artificial structures, and forced proof elements will be perceived as manipulative by Google's AI algorithms. E-E-A-T must be authentic, not performative.

## FAQ — Google E-E-A-T 2026

**How does Google really measure E-E-A-T?**
Google does not directly measure E-E-A-T — it is a conceptual framework. In practice, Google uses dozens of algorithmic signals that are proxies for these dimensions: backlink profile (authority), author structured data (expertise), reviews and mentions (trustworthiness), etc.

**Does E-E-A-T apply to small sites?**
Yes, but with a nuance: E-E-A-T standards are applied contextualizing the size and maturity of the site. A recent blog by a solo expert will not be judged with the same criteria as an institutional site established for 10 years.

**Can a recent site have good E-E-A-T?**
Yes. E-E-A-T is not solely tied to longevity. A recent site run by a recognized expert, with high-quality content and first quality backlinks, can have excellent E-E-A-T from its first weeks.

**Does generative AI have E-E-A-T?**
No — E-E-A-T is by definition attached to identifiable human authors and real organizations. This is precisely why AI-generated content must always be supervised and signed by a human author to benefit from that author's E-E-A-T.

**How does E-E-A-T affect GEO (visibility in generative AIs)?**
Strongly. Generative AIs tend to cite sources with strong E-E-A-T, as these sources correspond to the most represented sources in their training data. Investing in E-E-A-T simultaneously improves your Google SEO and your GEO in ChatGPT/Perplexity.

## Conclusion: E-E-A-T, the Most Durable SEO Investment

E-E-A-T is the antithesis of short-term SEO tactics. You cannot "buy" good experience, force expertise, or durably simulate authority. This framework forces content creators to invest in what really matters: being genuinely useful, transparent, and competent.

This is precisely why E-E-A-T withstands algorithmic updates — it captures the essence of what Google has always sought to reward: the best information sources for its users.

In 2026, with AI infiltrating all aspects of SEO, E-E-A-T becomes even more strategic. Brands that build authentic authority will be those that generative AIs cite, that Google promotes, and that users trust.
