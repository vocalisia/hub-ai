# AI for Energy and Climate — Sustainable Solutions and Smart Grid

Oslo, capital of Norway and the world's most advanced city in electric mobility, embodies the convergence between **artificial intelligence** and the energy transition. With over 80% of new cars sold being electric and nearly 100% hydroelectric power, Norway is leading the way — and AI is the invisible engine of this green revolution.

## Smart Grids: the intelligent electrical network

The traditional electrical grid, designed for unidirectional flow from power plants to consumers, is inadequate for the age of renewable energy. AI transforms this grid into a **smart grid** — an intelligent, flexible and resilient system.

### Managing intermittency

The main challenge of renewable energy is intermittency. AI solves this problem:

- **Solar production forecasting**: predicting photovoltaic output at 15 minutes, 1 hour and 24 hours, with 95% accuracy
- **Wind forecasting**: deep learning wind modeling integrating meteorological, topographic and historical data
- **Supply-demand balancing**: real-time adjustment of production, storage and consumption
- **Storage management**: optimization of charge/discharge cycles of large-scale batteries

### Intelligent Demand Response

AI orchestrates **demand flexibility**:

1. **Load shifting**: moving non-critical consumption (water heaters, washing machines, vehicle charging) to periods of renewable surplus
2. **Dynamic pricing**: real-time electricity price adjustment to balance the grid
3. **Virtual aggregation**: thousands of small prosumers managed as a single virtual power plant
4. **Micro-grids**: local autonomous networks capable of island operation in case of failure

### Predictive grid maintenance

AI detects problems before they cause outages:

- **Sensor data analysis**: thousands of sensors on lines, transformers and substations
- **Anomaly detection**: identifying degrading equipment before failure
- **Intervention optimization**: intelligent planning of maintenance work
- **Loss reduction**: detection of technical and non-technical losses (fraud)

## Renewable energy optimized by AI

### Smart solar

AI maximizes the yield of solar installations:

- **Dynamic orientation**: AI-optimized solar tracking for tracker-equipped panels
- **Defect detection**: drone and AI vision analysis of damaged or soiled panels
- **Production forecasting**: integration into grid planning
- **Optimal sizing**: designing installations adapted to actual consumption profiles

### Predictive wind power

Wind farms use AI to:

- **Optimize yaw**: orienting each turbine based on local wind conditions
- **Reduce wake effects**: coordinating turbines to maximize park output
- **Predict maintenance**: vibration and acoustic analysis to anticipate failures
- **Extend lifespan**: adapting operating parameters to reduce wear

### Smart hydroelectricity

Norway, with its fjords and dams, uses AI to optimize hydroelectricity:

- **Reservoir management**: balancing electricity production, flood management and ecological needs
- **Hydrological forecasting**: predicting water inflows based on weather and snowmelt
- **Coordination with intermittent renewables**: hydro as a natural battery to compensate for solar and wind variations

## Electric mobility and AI

Norway is the world leader in electric mobility, and AI is a key enabler. [Tesla-Mag](https://tesla-mag.ch) regularly covers advances in this field, and [smart mobility](/fr/blog/ia-mobilite-tesla) is a topic in full effervescence.

### Intelligent charging management

- **Charging schedule optimization**: charging during off-peak hours or renewable surpluses
- **Bidirectional charging (V2G)**: electric vehicles as distributed batteries for the grid
- **Trip planning**: integrating charging stations, real range and traffic conditions
- **Demand prediction**: optimal sizing and placement of charging stations

### Autonomous driving and energy efficiency

Autonomous vehicles, powered by AI, are also **more efficient** vehicles:

1. **Eco-routing**: choosing the most energy-efficient route, not just the fastest
2. **Predictive driving**: anticipating lights, slowdowns, slopes to optimize consumption
3. **Platooning**: automated truck convoys that reduce aerodynamic drag by 20%
4. **Shared mobility**: autonomous fleets that optimize vehicle utilization rates

## AI against climate change

Beyond energy, AI is a powerful tool against climate change:

### Environmental monitoring

- **Emission surveillance**: satellite monitoring of CO2 and methane emissions by AI
- **Deforestation**: real-time detection of deforestation through satellite image analysis
- **Air quality**: pollution forecasting and population alerts
- **Biodiversity**: species identification and tracking through AI-powered sound and visual recognition

### Climate modeling

AI dramatically improves climate models:

- **Increased resolution**: downscaling global models to local scale
- **More accurate forecasts**: integrating variables ignored by traditional models
- **Impact scenarios**: simulating warming consequences on ecosystems, agriculture, cities
- **Decision support**: visualization and simulation tools for policymakers

### Carbon capture and storage

AI optimizes carbon capture technologies:

- **Site identification**: finding optimal geological storage sites
- **Process optimization**: reducing the energy cost of capture
- **Integrity monitoring**: long-term monitoring of storage site integrity
- **DAC (Direct Air Capture)**: improving direct air capture processes

## Smart buildings and cities

AI transforms the energy management of buildings, which account for **40% of energy consumption** in Europe:

### Building Energy Management Systems (BEMS)

- **Heating and cooling**: automatic adjustment based on occupancy, forecast weather and energy tariffs
- **Smart lighting**: adaptation to natural light and presence
- **Optimized ventilation**: indoor air quality maintained with minimal consumption
- **Energy certification**: AI helps achieve BREEAM, LEED, Minergie standards

### AI-powered district heating

District heating networks, widespread in the [Nordic countries](/fr/blog/ia-scandinavie-nordics), are optimized by AI:

1. **Thermal demand forecasting** by building and by hour
2. **Network temperature optimization** to minimize losses
3. **Multi-source integration**: industrial waste heat, geothermal, biomass, heat pumps
4. **Thermal storage**: intelligent management of seasonal heat reservoirs

## AI-driven industrial decarbonization

The most carbon-intensive industrial sectors use AI to reduce their footprint:

- **Steelmaking**: optimizing blast furnaces to reduce coke consumption
- **Cement**: reducing CO2 emissions through clinkerization process optimization
- **Chemistry**: AI-driven design of more efficient catalytic processes
- **Data centers**: Google reduced cooling consumption in its data centers by 40% using DeepMind

[Trust in these AI solutions](https://trustly-ai.com) is essential for their mass adoption by industry.

## Green finance and AI

AI facilitates the financing of the energy transition:

- **ESG scoring**: automated assessment of the environmental impact of companies and projects
- **Green bonds**: automatic verification of fund usage for green bond issuances
- **Climate risk**: assessment of financial exposure to climate change risks
- **Carbon trading**: optimization of carbon credit buying and selling strategies

## The challenges of green AI

AI itself has an environmental impact that must not be ignored:

- **Energy consumption**: training GPT-4 consumed as much electricity as a city of 50,000 inhabitants for a month
- **Cooling water**: data centers consume massive quantities of water
- **Rare materials**: GPU components require rare earth elements
- **E-waste**: rapid hardware renewal generates electronic waste

The answer lies in **frugal AI**: more efficient models, optimized hardware, 100% renewable-powered data centers.

## Conclusion

Artificial intelligence is an indispensable accelerator of the energy and climate transition. From Oslo to the rest of Europe, smart grids, optimized renewables and intelligent buildings demonstrate that technology and sustainability are not incompatible — quite the contrary. AI may be our best tool for meeting the climate challenge, provided we ensure the solution does not itself become the problem.

The energy transition is a collective challenge — and AI is the lever that can accelerate everything.
